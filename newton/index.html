Liquid Termite Barriers

Liquid termiticide is applied to the soil around the foundation of your property, creating a protective barrier that termites cannot bridge. Termite Baiting Systems

Termite baiting systems involve placing bait stations around your property. Termites are attracted to the bait, which contains a slow-acting insect growth regulator. As termites consume the bait and return to their colony, they share the bait, eventually leading to the demise of the entire colony. This is an environmentally friendly and highly effective method for eliminating termite colonies.

Common Termite Problems and How We Solve Them

Termite infestations can manifest in various ways, and recognizing the signs early is crucial. Here are some common problems associated with termites and how Hickory Termite provides effective solutions.

Identifying Subterranean Termites

Subterranean termites are the most common and destructive type of termites in the Newton area. They live in underground colonies and build mud tubes to travel between their colony and their food source (your home). These mud tubes are often found on foundations, walls, or other structures and are a clear sign of an infestation. Ignoring these tubes can lead to significant structural damage over time. We specialize in detecting these subtle signs of subterranean termite activity before they escalate into major issues. Our inspection goes beyond superficial checks, delving into hidden areas where these elusive pests are likely to be found. By accurately identifying the presence and extent of subterranean termite colonies, we can implement targeted treatments that eliminate the infestation at its source, protecting your property from further damage. Early detection and intervention are vital when dealing with subterranean termites, as their colonies can grow rapidly and cause extensive harm in a relatively short period. Trust our experts to accurately diagnose the problem and implement the most effective solution.

Detecting Mud Tubes

Mud tubes are a key indicator of subterranean termites. These pencil-sized tubes are made of mud, soil, and wood particles and serve as pathways for termites to travel safely between their underground colony and the wood they are feeding on. Finding mud tubes on your foundation, walls, or in crawl spaces is a strong sign of a termite infestation. We train our technicians to meticulously search for these tubes in all potential locations around your property. Observing the size, location, and activity within these tubes helps us understand the scale and direction of the termite activity. Disturbing these tubes is not recommended, as it may cause the termites to simply create new tubes in other locations, making eradication more challenging. Instead, contact us immediately if you discover mud tubes, and our experts will conduct a professional assessment and develop a targeted treatment plan based on our findings. Our thorough inspection process ensures that we identify all active mud tubes and other signs of termite presence, guaranteeing a comprehensive approach to eliminating the infestation. We leave no stone unturned in our quest to protect your property from these destructive pests and the damage they cause. The presence of these tubes is a direct indication that termites are actively foraging for food, and immediate professional intervention is necessary to prevent further structural damage to your home or business.

Pinpointing Drywood Termite Infestations

Unlike subterranean termites, drywood termites do not require contact with the soil. They infest dry wood directly, often found in attics, furniture, or structural timbers. Signs of drywood termites include small piles of fecal pellets (frass) near infested wood and damaged wood that appears smooth inside. These infestations can be harder to detect in their early stages compared to subterranean termites because they don't build visible mud tubes. However, recognizing the presence of frass, which resembles sawdust or sand and is often found in small piles, is a crucial indicator of a drywood termite problem. We are skilled in recognizing the subtle signs of drywood termite activity, including the characteristic shape and appearance of their fecal pellets and the specific patterns of damage they inflict on wood. Our inspection process for drywood termites involves a detailed examination of wooden structures, furniture, and other potential infestation sites, paying close attention to areas where frass might accumulate. Early detection of drywood termites is important because while they may cause damage more slowly than subterranean termites, they can still compromise the structural integrity of your property over time if left untreated. Trust our experts to accurately diagnose a drywood termite infestation and provide the appropriate treatment to eliminate these pests and protect your property from further harm.

Recognizing Termite Swarms

Termite swarming is a natural part of the termite life cycle, where winged reproductive termites (swarmers) emerge from the colony to mate and establish new colonies. Seeing a swarm of winged insects, often in the spring or after rainfall, can be alarming and is a sure sign of a nearby termite colony. These swarms are often mistaken for flying ants, but there are distinct differences in their appearance. Termite swarmers have straight antennae, uniform wings that are all the same size (much longer than their bodies), and a broad waist, while flying ants have elbowed antennae, uneven wings, and a narrow waist. The presence of termite swarmers, or discarded wings near windowsills or light sources, indicates that a mature termite colony is active nearby and potentially expanding. While the swarmers themselves don't cause damage, their presence confirms the existence of a termite infestation that needs to be addressed. We can quickly identify whether you are dealing with termite swarmers or flying ants and provide expert guidance on the next steps. Seeing a swarm is a clear call to action to have your property inspected for a termite infestation. Our team is experienced in locating the source of the swarm and implementing effective treatment to eliminate the colony before it causes significant damage to your home or business. Don't ignore the signs of a termite swarm; it's your property's way of telling you there's a problem that needs professional attention. We can help you interpret these signs and take appropriate, timely action to protect your investment.

Addressing Termite Damage

Termites feed on cellulose, which is found in wood. This feeding results in damaged wood that can compromise the structural integrity of your home or business. Termite damage often appears as hollowed-out wood, blistering paint, or sagging floors. Recognizing the early signs of damage can prevent extensive and costly repairs. The damage inflicted by termites can range from minor surface damage to significant structural compromise, depending on the severity and duration of the infestation. In some cases, the damage may be hidden behind walls or under flooring, making it difficult to detect without a professional inspection. We are experienced in identifying various types and stages of termite damage, even in less obvious locations. Our inspectors use specialized tools and techniques to assess the extent of the damage caused by the infestation, providing you with a clear understanding of the situation. Once the termites have been eradicated, addressing the damage they've caused is the next step. We can provide recommendations for reputable contractors who specialize in repairing termite-damaged structures. Preventing further damage is our priority, and our treatment plans are designed not only to eliminate the existing termites but also to prevent future infestations, safeguarding your property's structural integrity over the long term. Early detection and prompt action are crucial in minimizing the amount and severity of termite damage, preserving the value and safety of your property.
